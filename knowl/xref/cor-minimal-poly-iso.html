Corollary 2.9. Minimal Polynomial Isomorphisms.

Let \(p \in F[x]\) be the minimal polynomial of \(\alpha\) for some \(\alpha \in L/F\text{.}\) Define \(\iota: F[x]/(p(x)) \rightarrow F(\alpha)\) by \(\iota\left(\overline{f(x)}\right) = f(\alpha)\text{.}\) By the First Isomorphism Theorem, \(\iota\) is an isomorphism and \(F[x]/(p(x)) \cong F(\alpha)\text{.}\) Additionally, we have \(\alpha \in \textrm{im}(\iota)\) and \(F(\alpha)\) is minimal among the fields containing \(F\) and \(\alpha\text{.}\)
🔗
In other words, if \(\alpha\) is algebraic, then \(F[x]/(p(x)) \cong F(\alpha)\) where \(p\) is the minimal polynomial for \(\alpha\) over \(F\text{.}\)
